## ch1 Owera Software (EN)

### 1. r/LocalLLaMA — comment (find a fresh thread on Cursor/Copilot pricing or model lock-in)
The thing that finally pushed me off the $20/mo closed coding tools wasn't price — it was
lock-in. The moment you want to swap in a local/OSS model or a cheaper provider, you can't:
the tool is welded to one backend. Model-agnostic agents (OpenCode-style, 75+ providers) flip
that — you keep your workflow and route the model however you want, and "free" stops meaning
"$0 until you actually use it."

I broke down the real monthly cost of the "free" route (spoiler: it's API spend, not zero) here:
https://youtube.com/watch?v=w2-hqrkDaPU — happy to compare notes on what people are actually
paying at heavy use.

### 2. dev.to — short post (security angle, genuinely strong)
**Title:** Your AI coding agent may have shipped your prompts to a third-party model by default

**Body:**
A recent AI coding agent release sent user prompts to an external model *by default* — no opt-in.
It's fixed now, but it's a useful reminder: an AI coding agent is an exfiltration path. Two things
worth doing today regardless of which tool you use — (1) check where your prompts and file context
actually go (read the network settings, not the marketing), and (2) assume anything you paste into
an agent could leave your machine.

I walked through what broke and how to check your own setup here:
https://youtube.com/watch?v=JkAzNajKtJs
