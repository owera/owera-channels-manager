## Triage — the one high-leverage issue, fixed
**ch2 was fully blocked from publishing.** Video **#448** (a stalling YouTube upload with no
HTTP timeout) sat in `publishing`, got recovered to `approved` every 900s timeout window,
was re-picked, and stalled again — forever. Because recovery flips it back to APPROVED right
before the channel loop (in-flight → 0), it defeated the in-flight guard and **one stuck
video blocked ALL of ch2's publishes** (0 published today at noon, 8 approved waiting;
`retry_count` had reached 18).

**Fix (commit `1d29a98`, pushed):**
- `publish_loop._recover_stuck_publishing`: re-queue at most `publish_max_retries` (5) times,
  then mark the video `failed` instead of re-queuing, so it stops hogging the drip.
- `config.publish_max_retries = 5` (new).
- `issues._failed_action`: a file-backed failed video that has **exhausted** publish retries is
  escalated (`auto=False`) rather than auto-retried into the same stall — strictly narrower;
  normal one-off publish failures still auto-retry.

**Verified:** isolated tests of `_recover_stuck_publishing` (below-cap→requeued; at-cap→failed;
fresh→untouched; over-cap #448→failed) and `_failed_action` (exhausted→retry=False; normal
1-fail→retry=True) all pass; `verify_storyboard` 40/40. **Live outcome (operator-confirmed):**
after the manager restart, #448 → `failed` ("upload repeatedly stalled … gave up after 20
attempts") and **ch2 publishing resumed** (a different video now in flight; the 8 approved are
draining; next publish 13:30 UTC).
