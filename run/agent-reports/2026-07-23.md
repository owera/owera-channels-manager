## The pipeline stall (the main event this run)

**Nothing in the app promotes DRAFT → QUEUED.** No scheduler job does it; only an explicit
`POST /api/videos/{id}/produce`. The render loop consumes **QUEUED** only. So production depends
entirely on the growth agent producing drafts each run.

The last five runs didn't. `board_inventory.at_capacity` counts **DRAFT + QUEUED** as "pending",
so a bench of 10 unproduced drafts reads `days_of_inventory: 2.0, at_capacity: true` — which the
capacity gate turns into "skip idea generation and trend adoption". With drafts never produced,
pending stayed pinned at 10 forever and the render loop had nothing to consume. Meanwhile:

- The issues digest reported `clean` throughout (only a near-cap quota row).

## Learned (winners / hypotheses)
1. **The board-capacity gate had an undetected failure mode**: it conflates "the bench is full" with
   "there is work in flight". A bench of pure DRAFTs satisfies the gate while starving the renderer.
   Fixed structurally, not by remembering to produce.
2. **ch1's conversion problem is real and is not a distribution problem.** 757 views, 82.7% from the
   Shorts feed, 1 subscriber. The algorithm is testing ch1's videos hard; viewers just don't subscribe.
3. **ch1's only converter is the "agents-explained" wedge pillar (MCP, 58.9% avp)**, while its
   top-viewed videos are the parked generic-tips topic converting zero. Hypothesis for coming runs:
   ch1 conversion tracks *retention depth*, not views — the >55% avp explainers convert, the
   35-40% avp tip videos don't, regardless of reach.
