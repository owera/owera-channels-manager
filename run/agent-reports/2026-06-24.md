### Bug: Publish-loop pileup (root cause fixed)

**Observed**: 286 "recovered stuck publish (>900s) — re-queued" errors over ~4.7 hours. Ch2 had 16 videos simultaneously in PUBLISHING state with 0 published today. Recovery loop fired every minute, which combined with 16 in-flight videos (each at a different phase of the 15-min timeout) produced ~1 error/min.

**Root cause**: Two compounding issues:
1. No guard prevented starting a new upload while one is already in-flight. APScheduler runs ticks in parallel threads; hung uploads let new ticks see 0 in-flight and pick the next APPROVED video.
2. The drip timer (`_drip_ok`) only counts **successful** publishes. Hung uploads never reach PUBLISHED, so drip always passes and the loop kept picking new videos.

**Fix applied** (`ee783bf`): Added a guard in `publish_loop.tick()` that skips publishing for a channel if any video is already in PUBLISHING state. One-line count check before `_next_approved`. Verified working: at time of check, ch2 drained from 16 to 10 PUBLISHING + 6 APPROVED (recovery draining, loop not adding new ones).

**After-state proof**: `GET /api/dashboard` shows `ch2: publishing=10, approved=6` — down from `publishing=16, approved=0` before fix. Recovery continues to drain; expected to reach `publishing=0, approved=16` within ~15 min.

**No escalation needed from triage** — operator should monitor ch2 publish success rate over next hour (see ⚠ block above).
